## 1. Unsecured Lending Limits (Circular RBI/2024/DL-07)

### Personal Loans
- Maximum unsecured personal loan limit for scheduled commercial banks: **₹25,00,000**
- For NBFCs and digital lenders, the cap is **₹15,00,000** without collateral
- Risk weight for unsecured consumer credit exposures exceeding ₹10,00,000: **125%**
- Risk weight for unsecured consumer credit exposures up to ₹10,00,000: **100%**
- Banks must maintain a minimum Capital to Risk-weighted Assets Ratio (CRAR) of **11.5%** for unsecured lending portfolio
- Total unsecured exposure of a bank shall not exceed **25% of total advances**

### Credit Card Limits
- Maximum credit limit on unsecured credit cards: **5x of monthly net income** for salaried individuals
- For self-employed individuals: **3x of average monthly income** (based on last 2 years ITR)
- Add-on card holders must be at least **18 years** old
- No unsolicited credit limit enhancements without explicit written consent from cardholder
- Minimum payment due shall be at least **5% of total outstanding** or ₹200, whichever is higher

### Restrictions
- Banks cannot grant unsecured loans to individuals with credit score below **600**
- Borrowers with more than **3 active unsecured loans** across all lenders require enhanced due diligence
- Unsecured loan tenure shall not exceed **60 months** (5 years)
- Top-up loans on existing unsecured facilities must be treated as fresh exposures for risk weighting

## 2. KYC Verification Guidelines (Master Direction RBI/2024/KYC-14)

### Tiered KYC Norms

| KYC Level | Max Balance | Max Annual Turnover | Verification Method |
|-----------|-------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Minimum KYC | ₹50,000 | ₹1,00,000 | OTP-based Aadhaar eKYC |
| Small KYC | ₹2,00,000 | ₹5,00,000 | Video KYC + Document Upload |
| Full KYC | No Limit | No Limit | In-person verification or CKYC |

### Mandatory Documents
- **PAN Card** — Mandatory for all accounts with annual transaction exceeding ₹2,50,000
- **Aadhaar** — Required for eKYC; optional for physical KYC but encouraged
- **Address Proof** — Any one of: Voter ID, Passport, Driving License, Utility Bill (< 3 months old)
- **Income Proof** (for loan products): Salary slips (3 months), Form 16, ITR (2 years for self-employed)
- **Bank Statements** — Last 6 months for loan applications exceeding ₹3,00,000

### CKYC (Central KYC Registry)
- All banks must upload KYC records to the CKYC registry within **10 business days** of account opening
- CKYC number is a **14-digit unique identifier** assigned to each individual
- Periodic KYC re-verification: Every **2 years** for high-risk customers, **8 years** for medium-risk, **10 years** for low-risk
- eKYC via Aadhaar is valid for **1 year** for SDD (Simplified Due Diligence) accounts, after which full KYC must be completed

### Video KYC (V-CIP)
- Video KYC must be conducted by a trained bank official, not outsourced agents
- Customer's live photograph must be captured and matched against ID proof
- Geo-tagging and IP logging mandatory during V-CIP session
- Entire session must be recorded and stored for **8 years**
- PAN verification via NSDL/UTIITSL is mandatory during V-CIP

## 3. EMI Repayment Regulations (Circular RBI/2024/CL-22)

### Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio (FOIR)
- Maximum permissible FOIR for personal loans: **50%** of net monthly income
- Maximum permissible FOIR for home loans: **65%** of net monthly income (including proposed EMI)
- For credit card EMI conversions: FOIR calculated on total outstanding EMI conversions + new conversion
- Joint borrowers: Combined FOIR computed on aggregate of both incomes

### EMI Bounce Penalties
- First EMI bounce penalty: Maximum **₹750** (inclusive of GST)
- Second consecutive bounce: Maximum **₹1,250**
- Third consecutive bounce: Account flagged for **SMA-0** classification; penalty up to **₹1,500**
- Banks cannot charge penal interest exceeding **2% per annum** above the contracted rate on overdue amounts
- As per RBI Circular dated 18 August 2023, penal charges must be levied as **penal charges, not penal interest**

### Prepayment Rules
- **No prepayment penalty** on floating-rate term loans to individual borrowers
- Fixed-rate personal loans: Prepayment penalty capped at **4% of principal outstanding** if prepaid within first 12 months
- Fixed-rate personal loans (after 12 months): Prepayment penalty capped at **2%**
- Home loans (floating rate): Zero foreclosure charges at any time
- Part-prepayment allowed without restriction for floating-rate loans; minimum part-prepayment amount: **₹10,000** or **one EMI**, whichever is higher

### Loan Moratorium
- Moratorium period for home loans: Up to **36 months** (principal moratorium)
- EMI holiday: Maximum **3 months** once in the loan tenure for personal loans (subject to lender approval)
- During moratorium, interest continues to accrue and is capitalized

## 4. Credit Score Requirements (Guideline RBI/2024/CS-03)

### Minimum Credit Score by Product Type

| Product | Minimum Credit Score | Risk Category |
|---------|---------------------|---------------|
| Personal Loan (< ₹5L) | 650 | Medium |
| Personal Loan (₹5L–₹15L) | 700 | Medium-High |
| Personal Loan (> ₹15L) | 750 | High |
| Home Loan | 650 | Low-Medium |
| Car Loan (New Vehicle) | 650 | Low-Medium |
| Car Loan (Used Vehicle) | 700 | Medium |
| Credit Card (Basic) | 650 | Medium |
| Credit Card (Premium) | 750 | High |
| Gold Loan | No minimum | Secured |
| Education Loan | 600 | Low |
| Business Loan (MSME) | 680 | Medium |

### Credit Bureau Reporting
- All loans and credit facilities ≥ ₹1,000 must be reported to at least **2 credit bureaus**
- Reporting frequency: **Monthly**, by the 15th of the following month
- Settlement status ("settled" vs "closed") must be accurately reported
- Borrowers have the right to **one free credit report per year** from each bureau
- Disputes must be resolved within **30 days** of complaint registration

### Credit Score Impact Factors

| Factor | Weight in Score | Description |
|--------|----------------|-------------|
| Repayment History | 35% | On-time payments across all credit lines |
| Credit Utilization | 30% | Ratio of used credit to total available credit |
| Credit Age | 15% | Average age of all credit accounts |
| Credit Mix | 10% | Diversity of credit types (secured + unsecured) |
| Recent Inquiries | 10% | Hard inquiries in last 6 months |

## 5. Interest Rate Policies (Master Direction RBI/2024/IR-09)

### External Benchmark Linked Lending Rate (EBLR)
- All floating-rate retail and MSME loans must be linked to an **external benchmark**
- Approved benchmarks: RBI Repo Rate, 3-month T-Bill yield, 6-month T-Bill yield, any other benchmark by FBIL
- **Current RBI Repo Rate**: 6.50% (as of fictional date)
- Banks can add a **spread** over the benchmark; spread fixed at time of sanction for the entire loan tenure
- Reset frequency: **Every 3 months** (minimum)
- Transmission of rate changes must happen within **3 months** of benchmark change

### Spread Caps by Product

| Product | Typical Spread (bps) | Effective Rate Range |
|---------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Home Loan | 175–350 bps | 8.25% – 10.00% |
| Personal Loan | 450–900 bps | 11.00% – 15.50% |
| Car Loan | 250–500 bps | 9.00% – 11.50% |
| Education Loan (domestic) | 100–300 bps | 7.50% – 9.50% |
| MSME Loan | 300–600 bps | 9.50% – 12.50% |
| Gold Loan | 200–400 bps | 8.50% – 10.50% |

### Fixed Rate Rules
- Fixed-rate loans: Interest rate cannot be changed unilaterally during the loan tenure
- Conversion from fixed to floating rate: Bank may charge a **conversion fee up to 2%** of outstanding principal
- Banks must clearly disclose whether the rate is **fixed, floating, or hybrid** at time of sanction

## 6. Digital Lending Compliance (RBI DL Guidelines 2023, Updated 2024)

### Mandatory Requirements
- All loan disbursements must be made **directly to the borrower's bank account** (not to third-party wallets/accounts)
- Loan Service Providers (LSPs) and Digital Lending Apps (DLAs) must prominently display the **name of the lending bank/NBFC**
- A **Key Fact Statement (KFS)** must be provided to the borrower before loan execution containing:
  - Annual Percentage Rate (APR)
  - Recovery mechanism
  - Cooling-off period details
  - Grievance redressal officer details

### Cooling-Off Period
- Borrower has a **3-day cooling-off period** from the date of disbursal to exit the loan without penalty
- For loans of tenure ≤ 7 days: Cooling-off period is **1 day**
- During cooling-off, borrower returns the principal + proportional interest only
- No processing fees, convenience fees, or charges can be retained if borrower exercises cooling-off

### Data Privacy
- DLAs can access **only these device permissions**: Camera (for KYC), Location (for geo-tagging), SMS (for bank statement parsing with consent)
- No access to contacts, photos, gallery, call logs, or storage
- Data retention: Customer data must be purged within **3 months** of loan closure or rejection
- Biometric data: Cannot be stored by the LSP; must be processed only via UIDAI Aadhaar authentication APIs

### FLDG (First Loss Default Guarantee)
- Maximum FLDG that an LSP can provide to a Regulated Entity: **5% of the loan portfolio**
- FLDG must be in the form of **cash deposits or bank guarantees**
- FLDG arrangements must be reviewed every **6 months**

## 7. Risk Classification Rules (NPA Norms — Circular RBI/2024/NPA-11)

### Asset Classification

| Category | Days Past Due (DPD) | Provisioning Requirement |
|----------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| Standard | 0 DPD | 0.40% (general provision) |
| SMA-0 (Special Mention Account) | 1–30 DPD | 0.40% |
| SMA-1 | 31–60 DPD | 5% |
| SMA-2 | 61–90 DPD | 10% |
| Sub-Standard (NPA) | 91–365 DPD | 15% |
| Doubtful (1 year) | 366–730 DPD | 25% |
| Doubtful (> 1 year) | > 730 DPD | 40% |
| Loss Asset | Identified as loss | 100% |

### Upgrade Criteria
- NPA can be upgraded to **Standard** only when **all arrears of interest and principal are paid** by the borrower
- For restructured accounts: 12-month satisfactory performance period before upgrade
- Agricultural loans: NPA classification after **2 crop seasons** (not 90 days)

### Reporting
- SMA-1 and SMA-2 accounts must be reported to **CRILC (Central Repository of Information on Large Credits)** within 7 days
- All NPAs > ₹5 crore must be reported individually to the RBI's Supervisory department
- Willful defaulters must be reported to the RBI and credit bureaus within **30 days** of classification

## 8. Fair Practices Code for Lenders (Guideline RBI/2024/FPC-06)

### Loan Application Processing
- Banks must communicate loan approval/rejection within **30 days** of receiving a complete application
- Rejection must include the **specific reason(s)** for rejection in writing
- Processing fee is refundable if the bank rejects the loan application
- Loan agreement must be provided in the **language understood by the borrower**

### Interest Rate Communication
- Banks must clearly communicate the **Effective Annual Rate (EAR)** to the borrower
- All charges (processing fee, insurance, stamp duty, documentation charges) must be disclosed upfront
- Rate of interest and the approach for charging interest must be part of the **loan sanction letter**

### Recovery Practices
- Recovery agents must carry a valid **authorization letter** from the bank
- Recovery calls only between **8:00 AM and 7:00 PM**
- No intimidation, verbal abuse, or threats during recovery
- If a borrower raises a complaint about harassment, bank must investigate within **15 days**
- Use of physical force, criminal intimidation, or defamation during recovery is a criminal offence

### Grievance Redressal
- Every branch must display the name and contact of the **Grievance Redressal Officer**
- Complaints must be acknowledged within **3 business days**
- Resolution within **30 days** of complaint registration
- If unresolved, borrower can escalate to the **Banking Ombudsman** (RBI Integrated Ombudsman Scheme)
- No charge for filing a complaint with the Ombudsman

## 9. Lending to Priority Sector (Guideline RBI/2024/PSL-18)

### Targets
- Domestic scheduled commercial banks must allocate **40% of Adjusted Net Bank Credit (ANBC)** to Priority Sector Lending
- Of this, **10% of ANBC** must go to weaker sections (women, SC/ST, minorities, small farmers)
- **8% of ANBC** to Small and Marginal Farmers
- **7.5% of ANBC** to Micro Enterprises

### Eligible Categories
- Agriculture: Crop loans up to **₹5,00,000** per farmer per season
- MSME: Loans up to **₹5 crore** for manufacturing, **₹2 crore** for services
- Education: Loans up to **₹20,00,000** for studies in India
- Housing: Loans up to **₹35,00,000** in metro cities, **₹25,00,000** in non-metro areas
- Social Infrastructure: Loans up to **₹10 crore** for schools, hospitals, drinking water, sanitation

### Incentives & Penalties
- Shortfall in PSL targets: Bank must contribute to **RIDF (Rural Infrastructure Development Fund)** maintained by NABARD
- RIDF deposits earn interest at **bank rate minus 3.5 percentage points**
- PSL Certificates (PSLCs) can be traded on the e-Kuber platform to meet shortfall
- Over-achievement in PSL generates tradeable PSLC certificates valid for the financial year

## 10. Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Compliance (Guideline RBI/2024/AML-20)

### Cash Transaction Reporting (CTR)
- All cash transactions exceeding **₹10,00,000** (single or aggregate in a month) must be reported to **FIU-IND**
- Cross-border wire transfers exceeding **₹5,00,000** must be reported
- Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) must be filed within **7 days** of detection

### Customer Due Diligence (CDD)
- Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) for:
  - Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) — domestic and foreign
  - Customers from high-risk jurisdictions (FATF grey/black list)
  - Non-face-to-face customers
  - Accounts with unusually high turnover relative to declared income
- Wire transfers > ₹50,000: Originator and beneficiary information must be included
- Record retention: All CDD records and transaction data must be maintained for **5 years** after account closure

### Penalties
- Non-compliance with AML norms: Monetary penalty up to **₹1 crore** per violation
- Repeated violations: Restriction on branch expansion, prohibition on accepting deposits
- Individual officers found negligent: Personal penalties and possible criminal prosecution under PMLA
